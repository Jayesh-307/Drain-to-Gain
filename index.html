Environmental Time-Series Analysis Platform

"Drain to Gain" is an engineering research platform designed to automate the forecasting of environmental and rainwater parameters. By moving beyond traditional Excel capabilities, this system leverages programmatic analysis to accurately predict key water quality indicator trends, promoting sustainable resource planning and water reuse.

Monitored Parameters

Our forecasting model tracks and analyzes five key chemical and physical metrics vital for rainwater recycling suitability:

  • Average Rainfall (mm) - Quantity and seasonal patterns.
  • pH Level - Standard acidity/alkalinity balance.
  • Turbidity (NTU) - Water clarity index.
  • Dissolved Oxygen (mg/L) - Biological viability metrics.
  • Nitrates (mg/L) - Organic runoff pollutant indicator.

The ARIMA Mathematical Model

ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) is a linear time-series forecasting model characterized by three order parameters: (p, d, q).

General Formula:
Φp(L)(1 - L)d Yt = Θq(L) εt
AR (p)
AutoRegressive: Relies on the relationship between an observation and a number of lagged observations.
I (d)
Integrated: The level of differencing applied to make the time-series stationary (removing trend/seasonality).
MA (q)
Moving Average: Leverages the dependency between an observation and residual errors from past lagged forecasts.
